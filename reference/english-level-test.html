The Scale

What is CEFR?

The Common European Framework of Reference for Languages sorts language ability into six levels, from A1 for beginners to C2 for near-native mastery. They group into three bands: Basic (A1, A2), Independent (B1, B2), and Proficient (C1, C2). Each level is defined by what you can actually do with the language, not by a single test.

A1A2B1B2C1C2

The framework was created by the Council of Europe and is now used worldwide. This quick test is an informal, playful nod to it, not an official exam.
